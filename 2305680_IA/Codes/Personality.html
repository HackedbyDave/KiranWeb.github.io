My Mentor

The Right Excellent Marcus Mosiah Garvey

Marcus Mosiah Garvey was a Jamaican political activist, the founder and first President-General of the Universal Negro Improvement Association and African Communities League,
through which he declared himself Provisional President of Africa. Garvey would've spent most of this life advocating for rights of Negros whether Jamaicans, Africans or Afro-Americans
and through this he was awarded Jamaica's first National Hero. He was a man of of many quotes, that from the countless uplifting speeches, books and
interviews he gave during his lifetime but the one that stood out to me was "If you have no confidence in self, you are twice defeated in the race of life."
Now why did I choose MMG as a mentor? This is simply due to me believing that we should stand up as one and unite to achieve a common goal or
to overcome whatever obstacles that are thrown in front of us throughout life's journey, and this is what he stood for.




Keirsey Temperament Sorter Personality Test








After taking the Keirsey Temperament Sorter, I found that the results resonated with many aspects of my personality, highlighting my strengths and communication style.
This suggests that the test has some validity for me personally. However, I also recognize that personality is complex and can change over time, so while the test provides useful insights,
it shouldn't be seen as a definitive assessment of who I am. Overall, it serves as a helpful tool for self-reflection rather than a complete picture.
